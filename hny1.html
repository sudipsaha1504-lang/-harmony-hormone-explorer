Explore Human Hormones

Dopamine

Dopamine is the reward hormone that drives motivation and pleasure.

Dopamine motivates behavior and creates a feeling of reward when we complete tasks or achieve goals. It spikes during activities like gaming, social media, eating, and achieving success.

Serotonin

Serotonin regulates mood, sleep, appetite, and emotional stability.

Unlike dopamine spikes, serotonin provides a long-term sense of contentment and wellbeing. It increases with sunlight, exercise, social bonding, and meaningful life progress.

Oxytocin

Known as the "love hormone".

Oxytocin strengthens trust, bonding, and relationships. It increases during hugs, affection, childbirth, and social bonding.

Cortisol

Cortisol is the stress hormone.

Cortisol helps the body respond to danger or stress, increasing alertness and energy. However chronic cortisol leads to anxiety, fatigue, and health problems.

Adrenaline

Adrenaline triggers the fight-or-flight response.

It prepares the body for immediate action by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy availability.

Testosterone

Testosterone is the primary male sex hormone.

It plays a key role in muscle development, bone density, and libido. It prepares the body for immediate action by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy availability.

Estrogen

Estrogen is a major female reproductive hormone produced mainly in the ovaries.

It plays a crucial role in the development of female physical characteristics such as breast development, body shape, and reproductive organ function. Estrogen also regulates the menstrual cycle and supports bone health, skin elasticity, and cardiovascular health. Although it is primarily associated with females, small amounts are also present in males and contribute to overall hormonal balance.
